Most of the film that comes through our door is in reasonably good shape. It needs careful cleaning, a few splices, and a scan that does the image justice, and that is exactly what it gets, at a quality that competes with any other high-end scanner working today. We handle both on the same equipment, to the same standard, because the gentle path that rescues a damaged reel is the same path that protects a healthy one.

Film other labs declined
The difference

Film others can’t scan

Most scanners move film by its perforations, the sprocket holes along the edge. On healthy film that works fine. But film that is shrunken, warped, brittle, or has torn perforations cannot survive that kind of handling, which is why other labs send it back.

The patented Reflex film scanners were developed specifically for that film. They handle even severely deteriorated stock with gentle, even motion and minimal contact: no jarring stop-start movement, no stress on fragile or damaged edges, and nothing touching the image itself. That is how we routinely scan reels conventional machines cannot even thread, including film shrunk as much as 23 percent, far beyond the point where standard scanners give up.

The result is archival-quality, frame-accurate digital scans from film that was, by every other measure, unscannable.

FAQ

Questions we hear most

What film gauges do you scan?

Regular 8mm, Super 8mm, Regular 16mm, Super 16mm, and 35mm, silent or with sound.

My film is shrunken, warped, or has vinegar syndrome. Can you still scan it?

In most cases, yes. Our scanners move film gently and do not depend on its perforations, so we routinely scan reels conventional machines cannot even thread, including film shrunk as much as 23 percent. Standard scanners usually lose the ability to transport film at around 2.5 percent. Send us a photo and we will tell you what is possible.

Will scanning damage my film?

No. The process is built around protecting the original. Nothing touches the image, and your film comes back in the same condition it arrived.

Do you clean and repair film before scanning?

Yes. Every reel is inspected, cleaned, and repaired by hand before it is scanned. That is part of the work, not an add-on.

What will I receive?

Archival masters as ProRes or DPX sequences, plus viewing copies for easy sharing. We recommend the right deliverables for how you plan to use the footage.

What resolution should I choose?

HD, 2K, or 4K, depending on the gauge and how the footage will be used. We always capture above what the format itself holds, so nothing in the original is lost, and we are glad to advise.

Does damaged film cost more?

Usually a little. Difficult film is scanned more slowly and carefully, which takes more time, but it is a modest difference, not a multiple. Every project is quoted individually.

Can you restore the footage, not just scan it?

Yes. Restoration and color correction are available as separate services, quoted by condition. We digitize first, then restore whatever you want restored.

Send us a photo and we will tell you what is possible.
